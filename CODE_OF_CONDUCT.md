# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

AEGIS is built in the open to make the internet safer. We want the community around it  contributors, maintainers, users, and researchers  to be just as safe, welcoming, and respectful as the experience we're trying to build for users.

We are committed to making participation in the AEGIS project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This Code of Conduct applies to everyone who participates in our spaces, and we expect it to be honored in good faith.

## Expected Behavior

We ask everyone in the community to:

- Be respectful, considerate, and welcoming, especially to newcomers.
- Assume good intent, and give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the project and the people it protects.
- Use clear, professional language and stay on topic.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences, and disagree without being disagreeable.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologize to those affected, and learn from them.
- Respect privacy  both users' and fellow contributors'.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following are not tolerated in any AEGIS space:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment, including sustained disruption of discussions.
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission ("doxxing").
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Handling Sensitive Content Responsibly

AEGIS works on detecting harmful and exploitative advertising, so some contributors  particularly those building or evaluating detection modules  may handle sensitive material. In addition to the rules above, everyone working with such content must:

- **Never** create, store, share, or transmit child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any AEGIS context. If such material is encountered, exclude it immediately and report it to **NCMEC** (USA, `report.cybertip.org`) or the appropriate national authority. This obligation is absolute and overrides any other instruction.
- Handle adult, violent, or otherwise distressing material professionally and only as strictly necessary for the work  never gratuitously, and never in shared or public channels.
- Respect the welfare of people doing annotation or review work: support reasonable session limits, breaks, and the right to opt out at any time.
- Never use community spaces to share, request, or normalize harmful content.

These expectations protect both the people in our community and the people AEGIS exists to defend.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all AEGIS project spaces  repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, the wiki, chat, and any other forum  and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

When reporting, please include (where you can): what happened, when and where, who was involved, any context or records (links, screenshots), and whether it is ongoing. You may report anonymously, though that can limit our ability to follow up.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and will take fair, proportionate action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. They may remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other interactions that violate this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons when appropriate.

We will generally follow these escalating responses, choosing the level that fits the impact:

1. **Correction**  a private, written note explaining the issue and why it was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning**  a warning with consequences for continued behavior, and a defined period of no interaction with those involved. Further violations may lead to a ban.
3. **Temporary ban**  a temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period.
4. **Permanent ban**  a permanent ban from any public interaction within the community, for sustained or severe violations.

Decisions can be appealed by contacting the maintainers with a concise description of your grievance; appeals are reviewed by maintainers not involved in the original decision where possible.
